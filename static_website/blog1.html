Why Should the Food Supply Chain Go Digital?

Digital India has become a vision for India. Everything should be accessible at the tip of one's finger, including the most recent advances in science and technology. These were the remarks used by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2015 to introduce the Digital India initiative.Digitalization is the transformation of a business model using digital technologies in order to increase revenue and value-added opportunities. It is the transition to a digital business model. Due to its ability to significantly alter how organisations operate by enhancing their internal business processes, it has swept the corporate world by storm.


Numerous opportunities for innovation have arisen as a result of the digital revolution of business in all sectors.We observe a full reinvention of the products, services, and experiences that are offered to the market in every industry, from retail to life sciences, from medicines to banking.Businesses need to understand that, in today's world, they must use digital channels to interact with their key stakeholders.


Indian farmers and stakeholders' lives could be significantly impacted by digital technologies, which will have the ability to completely change the country's agricultural industry. Unsustainable resource use, diminishing farm production, and the quickly rising demand for nutritious food are some of the main issues facing Indian agriculture.
